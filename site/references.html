Extra concept source used in this revision

This revision also pulled teaching concepts from the STAPL Skeleton Framework paper in the LCPC 2014 proceedings volume. The most important extracted ideas were STAPL's PARAGRAPH data flow engine, asynchronous RMIs on shared objects, parametric dependencies, and the use of point-to-point graph composition to avoid unnecessary global synchronization.

How to read the material here

  • Statements about exact implementation details should still be checked against the current STAPL repo and docs.
  • Comparisons to school topics are teaching analogies, not formal claims made by the upstream project.
  • Where the site infers a concept, it does so from the public examples and descriptions rather than private source commentary.
